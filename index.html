The European Union and the environment: A never-ending story

The European Union and environmental goals

The earth is warming up and getting hot. No wonder that the environment is a hot topic. Though this warming up is stressful, the politicians in the European Union keep a cool head. They try to cool down the environment by formulating targets with a strict deadline. The greenhouse gas emission needs to be lowered and renewable energy production needs to be increases. Oh, and not to forget: it really is cool now to recycle waste. No pressure. You would think that the European Union is getting a bit hot with all these goals. But the European Union has a way to keep its cool: Pointing fingers at eachother. If no one reaches the goals, will everyone be punsihed? Probably not.

The application at the bottom of this page helps the European Union to keep an eye on each other and see whether they perform best or worst on the three mentioned environmental variables: waste, ernergy and emission. But more important is that it helps environmental acvtivists to see which country really needs to catch up with the others and which country really has improved on recycling, gas emission and renewable energy during the last few years.

How to use this application

Be aware while using this application that it is positive and amazing if the amount of recycled waste and produced renewable energy is high. But: Having a lot of gas emission is negative and terrible!

Step 1: The map

To evaluate the countries, I established an index for each of the countries. For every variable, one point could be received. A country yields one full point if it performs best (or a value of zero in the case of the gas emission variable, since this ) on that variable compared to the other countries. So the index gives a relative score. If data was not available, then the country is punished with the minimal amount of points on this variable. When a country is clicked on, the radar chart is updated for this country.

Step 2: The variables menu

The circle menu has more information on the year 2015 for the country selected in the map. A full circle indicates that a country performs best per capita (or worst per capita in case of the emission) on this variable. The development of the selected variable in the circle menu will be shown in for the selected country in the map in the stacked graph at the right bottom of the page.

The radar chart: A comparison of the performance of countries in 2015

In the radar chart, the relative performance on the different variables can be compared for different countries. Again, the score relative to the best (or worst in case of emission) performing country is displayed. The dropdown menu allows to choose between different coutnries. No more than 4 countries can be compared at the same time. When clicking on the map, the radar chart will be updated for that selected country.

The stacked graph: An insight into the development of a country on one of the variables

The stacked graph shows the performance of a country on one of the three variables during the last years. It is activitated when a country and a variable have been clicked on in step 1 and step 2. The exact values are shown on hover.

And not to forget: A button to reset the values to the European average in 2015!
